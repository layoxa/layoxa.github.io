The challenge

Most dental "patient apps" are
marketing skins. Not products.

SmileLink set out to fix the actual gap — patients can't see their upcoming visits, message the office without phone tag, or get reminders that pull from real PMS data. Clinics, meanwhile, are juggling separate systems for SMS, email, scheduling, and records — none of which talk to each other.

The brief: native mobile apps for patients, a real clinic dashboard for staff, and first-class integration with OpenDental and Dentrix — without giving up the HIPAA-grade data discipline a real healthcare product requires.

What we shipped

One stack. Three surfaces.

Native mobile apps

iOS and Android — appointment view, two-way SMS, encrypted chat with the clinic, push reminders, and account management. Shipped to the App Store and Play Store.

Clinic dashboard

Role-based access for front desk, hygienists, and dentists. Multi-operatory booking, customizable SMS/email/push templates, and patient lookup that's actually fast.

PMS data warehouse

Custom integrations with OpenDental and Dentrix. Bidirectional sync, HIPAA-aligned storage, audit logging, and per-environment isolation (staging + production).

How we built it

Production-grade from day one.

SmileLink isn't a prototype. The data layer was designed for HIPAA compliance from the schema up: row-level isolation per clinic, encrypted PHI fields, audit trails on every read and write, and BAAs available for downstream subprocessors.

  • Mobile: native iOS (Swift) + Android (Kotlin), shared design system
  • Backend: Node + Postgres, end-to-end encrypted messaging layer
  • Integrations: OpenDental custom adapter, Dentrix bridge, Twilio for SMS
  • DevOps: CI/CD pipeline, multiple staging environments, blue/green deploys
  • Compliance: HIPAA-aligned storage, BAA available, role-based access control
