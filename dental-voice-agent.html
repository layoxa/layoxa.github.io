What it does

An AI receptionist that
actually books the appointment.

Most dental AI voice agents on the market are voicemail with better grammar. They capture intent, send the clinic an email, and call themselves a "callback bot." That isn't the bar.

A production dental AI voice agent writes the confirmed booking into the clinic's Open Dental schedule. It honors operatory rules, provider preferences, and appointment-type templates. It SMSes the patient a confirmation. And it logs the entire interaction in a HIPAA-aligned audit trail.

That's what we shipped with DDS Marketing — and it's the baseline for any dental AI voice agent that's worth deploying.

Capabilities

What our dental voice agent handles.

24/7 inbound call answering

Picks up every call — including after hours, lunch, and weekends. No voicemail, no hold music. After-hours bookings stop leaking to the next clinic on the Google list.

Real Open Dental booking

Writes confirmed appointments directly into the clinic's schedule — respecting operatory rules, provider preferences, and appointment-type templates. Same for Dentrix via our local bridge.

Insurance & pricing Q&A

Answers from a clinic-specific knowledge base ("Do you take Aetna PPO?", "How much is a cleaning without insurance?"). Not from a generic web crawl.

Post-call SMS & intake

Confirmation SMS, intake form links, recall reminders — all sent automatically through Twilio with a HIPAA-aligned messaging layer.

Observable, auditable

Every call produces a transcript, a structured summary, and intent resolution stats. Visible in a slim dashboard — you can audit exactly which step misfired when something goes sideways.

The stack

Built on a production-grade voice stack.

Voice technology is mostly solved now. What separates a real dental voice agent from a demo is the integration depth around it. Here's the stack we run:

  • OpenAI Realtime API — for low-latency speech-to-speech with tool-calling, so the agent can do things, not just talk
  • ElevenLabs voices — natural voice synthesis, clinic-specific voice personality
  • Twilio Voice + SMS — telephony layer with a signed BAA, plus outbound SMS confirmations
  • Open Dental + Dentrix adapters — real schedule reads and writes, not API illusions
  • Per-clinic knowledge base — insurance plans, providers, hours, policies — curated, not crawled
  • HIPAA-aligned data flow — encryption in transit and at rest, audit logs, BAA stack

FAQ

Dental voice agent — common questions.

What is a dental AI voice agent?

A dental AI voice agent is software that answers a clinic's inbound phone calls using natural-language AI. It greets the caller, captures intent, books or reschedules appointments directly in Open Dental or Dentrix, handles insurance Q&A, and hands off to staff when needed. The best dental voice agents run 24/7 and integrate with the practice's existing PMS.

Does it actually book appointments, or just take messages?

Production dental AI voice agents like the one we built for DDS Marketing write confirmed bookings directly into the clinic's Open Dental schedule, respecting operatory availability and appointment-type rules. The voice agent then SMSes the patient a confirmation and creates a handoff summary for front-desk staff.

Is a dental AI voice agent HIPAA compliant?

Leyoxa's dental voice agent is HIPAA-aligned: telephony runs through Twilio with a signed BAA, model providers run under BAAs, PHI is encrypted in transit and at rest, and every call produces an audit log. Read our HIPAA-compliant patient app checklist for the engineering detail.

How many calls can it handle end-to-end?

In Leyoxa's DDS Marketing deployment, the voice agent handles ~60–70% of inbound calls end-to-end in week one, climbing to 80–90% after a few rounds of knowledge-base tuning. After-hours numbers are higher because after-hours calls skew toward simpler intents. Read the full blog post for the breakdown.
